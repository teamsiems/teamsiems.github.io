Keep your eyes & ears open for CISPA. Beware of any bill that has "Protection" in the title, and is "for other purposes."

Just because SOPA and PIPA, the infamous internet "kill switch" bills, are largely dead does not mean the threat to internet free speech has become any less serious. The Cyber Intelligence Sharing and Protection Act CISPA, also known as <a href="http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/112/hr3523" target="_blank" title="Status of H.R. 3523">H.R. 3523</a>, is the latest mutation of these internet censorship and spying bills to hit the U.S. Congress -- and unless the American people speak up now to stop it, CISPA could lead to far worse repercussions for online free speech than SOPA or PIPA ever would have. Source: [SOPA mutates into much worse CISPA, the latest threat to internet free speech](http://www.naturalnews.com/035638_CISPA_SOPA_free_speech.html).

**UPDATE**:  
The House of Representatives passed the Cyber Intelligence Sharing and Protection Act (CISPA) (H.R. 3523) with a 248-168 vote on April 26. The silver lining is that the Obama administration issued a veto threat against CISPA if kept in its current form. Now, there are two bills before the Senate that deal with cyber security, "SECURE IT" (<a href="http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/112/s2151" target="_blank" title="Status of S. 2151">S. 2151</a>) and "Cybersecurity Act of 2012" (<a href="http://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/112/s2105" target="_blank" title="Status of S. 2105">S. 2105</a>). Source: [CISPA passes the House, privacy battle moves to Senate](http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-tech/post/cispa-passes-the-house-privacy-battle-moves-to-senate/2012/04/27/gIQA7cJBlT_blog.html), The Washington Post.

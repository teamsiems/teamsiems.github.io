[Artemis](https://www.nasa.gov/humans-in-space/artemis/), Greek goddess of the Moon, will follow in twin brother Apollo's footsteps, and return people to the Moon.
Beginning with the Artemis Accords in 2020, NASA and 7 other countries established the foundations to explore the Moon, Mars, and beyond. Since 2020 there has been much planning, research, and development. They planned the first 5 missions to hopefully begin building a Moon base.

[![](https://res.cloudinary.com/teamsiems/images/v1775663320/ArtemisI-patch/ArtemisI-patch.png?_i=AA)](https://res.cloudinary.com/teamsiems/images/v1775663320/ArtemisI-patch/ArtemisI-patch.png?_i=AA)

[Artemis I](https://www.nasa.gov/mission/artemis-i/): Successfully completed as an uncrewed test flight of the Space Launch System (SLS) rocket and Orion spacecraft. It launched from Kennedy Space Center on November 16, 2022, and splashed down December 11, 2022.

[![](https://res.cloudinary.com/teamsiems/images/v1775662469/ArtemisII-Crew-Patch_9366becdd/ArtemisII-Crew-Patch_9366becdd.jpg?_i=AA)](https://res.cloudinary.com/teamsiems/images/v1775662469/ArtemisII-Crew-Patch_9366becdd/ArtemisII-Crew-Patch_9366becdd.jpg?_i=AA)

[Artemis II](https://www.nasa.gov/mission/artemis-ii/): Successfully completed first crewed flight to the Moon since 1972! It launched from Kennedy Space Center on April 1, 2026, and splashed down in the Pacific Ocean April 10, 2026. "The first crewed Artemis flight marks a key step toward long‑term return to the Moon and future missions to Mars." - NASA

[![Artemis II crew takes picture of Earthset.](https://res.cloudinary.com/teamsiems/images/v1775655086/ArtemisII-Earthset-202604006/ArtemisII-Earthset-202604006.jpg?_i=AA)](https://res.cloudinary.com/teamsiems/images/v1775655086/ArtemisII-Earthset-202604006/ArtemisII-Earthset-202604006.jpg?_i=AA)
[Artemis III](https://www.nasa.gov/mission/artemis-iii/): Set to launch in 2027. NASA's Artemis III mission in low Earth orbit will test integrated operations between the Orion spacecraft and one or both commercial landers from SpaceX and Blue Origin respectively.
[Artemis IV](https://www.nasa.gov/mission/artemis-iv/): Set to launch in 2028. Humanity's return to the lunar surface.
Artemis V: Set to launch in 2028.

The [predictions for the 2020 hurricane season](https://teamsiems.com/2020/04/prediction-for-2020-hurricane-season/) were for an above-average hurricane season. Philip Klotzbach predicted 16 named storms, 8 hurricanes, and 4 major (Cat 3+) hurricanes this year; updated to 24 named storms, 12 hurricanes, and 5 major hurricanes. Hurricane season usually runs from June 1 through November 30 - although given the predictions it may extend beyond those boundaries.
From the [National Hurricane Center](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/), the 2020 Atlantic [tropical cyclone names](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/aboutnames.shtml) are: [Arthur](#Arthur), [Bertha](#Bertha), [Cristobal](#Cristobal), [Dolly](#Dolly), [Edouard](#Edouard), [Fay](#Fay), [Gonzalo](#Gonzalo), [Hanna](#Hanna), [Isaias](#Isaias), [Josephine](#Josephine), [Kyle](#Kyle), [Laura](#Laura), [Marco](#Marco), [Nana](#Nana), [Omar](#Omar), [Paulette](#Paulette), [Rene](#Rene), [Sally](#Sally), [Teddy](#Teddy), [Vicky](#Vicky), [Wilfred](#Wilfred).
In the event that more than twenty-one named tropical cyclones occur in the Atlantic basin in a season, additional storms will take names from the [Greek alphabet](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/aboutnames_history.shtml#greek): [Alpha](#Alpha), [Beta](#Beta), [Gamma](#Gamma), [Delta](#Delta), [Epsilon](#Epsilon), [Zeta](#Zeta), [Eta](#Eta), [Theta](#Theta), [Iota](#Iota), Kappa, Lambda, Mu, Nu, Xi, Omicron, Pi, Rho, Sigma, Tau, Upsilon, Phi, Chi, Psi, Omega.
## Arthur

[![Arthur first storm of 2020](https://res.cloudinary.com/teamsiems/images/w_150,h_150,c_fill,g_auto/v1603993167/Arthur-first-storm-2020_5843f81bc/Arthur-first-storm-2020_5843f81bc.png?_i=AA){: .img-fluid }](https://res.cloudinary.com/teamsiems/images/v1603993167/Arthur-first-storm-2020_5843f81bc/Arthur-first-storm-2020_5843f81bc.png?_i=AA)

Continuing the trend of the last 10 years the first named storm has formed before June 1 - the official start of the hurricane season. On Saturday, May 16, 2020, [Tropical Storm Arthur](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/archive/2020/ARTHUR.shtml?) formed off of Florida's east coast.
> Tropical Storm Arthur formed on Saturday night off the coast of Florida, making it the first named storm of the 2020 Atlantic hurricane season. Now, forecasters say the storm has its sights set on eastern North Carolina.
>
> Although the official start of hurricane season is June 1, there has been a preseason tropical system for most of the last 10 years, so it is not uncommon to have tropical activity this early, according to AccuWeather Meteorologist Courtney Travis.
>
> Source: [UPI.com](https://upi.com/7007694)
\*\*UPDATE 5/18/2020 1500 UTC\*\*: Tropical Storm Arthur is located about 20 miles east-southeast of [Cape Hatteras, North Carolina](https://goo.gl/maps/iX8e1yyYWfKgoS4P8) with winds of 50 miles per hour and moving north-northeast at 16 miles per hour.
A turn toward the northeast is expected later today, followed by a turn toward the east on Tuesday. A slower southeast or south-southeast motion is forecast to begin Tuesday night and continue through Wednesday.
## Bertha
A quickly forming morning storm turned into [Tropical Storm Bertha](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/archive/2020/BERTHA.shtml?) before it weakened into a Tropical Depression.
> Bertha made landfall with maximum sustained winds of 50 mph just to the east of Charleston, South Carolina, Wednesday at 9:30 a.m. EDT, according to the National Hurricane Center.
>
> Source: [Weather Underground](https://www.wunderground.com/article/storms/hurricane/news/2020-05-27-tropical-storm-bertha-forecast-landfall-south-north-carolina)
\*\*UPDATE 5/27/2020 2100 UTC\*\*: Tropical Depression Bertha is located about 55 miles east-northeast of [Columbia, South Carolina](https://goo.gl/maps/xnhXaMgj2WLcZU8N6) with winds of 30 miles per hour and moving north-northwest at 15 miles per hour. Additional weakening is expected, and Bertha is forecast to degenerate to a remnant low pressure area on Thursday.
Bertha is expected to produce total rain accumulation of 2 to 4 inches with isolated totals of 8 inches across northeastern South Carolina into west central to far southeastern North Carolina and southwest Virginia. Given very saturated antecedent conditions, this rainfall may produce life threatening flash flooding, aggravate and prolong ongoing river flooding, and produce rapid out of bank rises on smaller rivers.

## Edouard
[Tropical Storm Edouard](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/archive/2020/EDOUARD.shtml?) formed Sunday night, July 6, in far North Atlantic. It's moving quickly northeastward at 36 miles per hour with maximum sustained gusts of 40 miles per hour. It was located about 530 miles south of Cape Race, Newfoundland.
> Colorado State University hurricane research scientist Phil Klotzbach [said on Twitter](https://twitter.com/philklotzbach/status/1279969329732149248) that Edouard is the earliest fifth Atlantic named storm on record. The previous record was Emily, which formed on July 12, 2005.
>
> Source: [FoxNews.com](https://www.foxnews.com/world/tropical-storm-edouard-atlantic-hurricane-season-history-formation-weather)
\*\*UPDATE 7/7/2020 2100 UTC\*\*: Post-Tropical Cyclone Edouard is located about 445 miles southeast of Cape Race, Newfoundland with winds of 45 miles per hour moving northeast at 38 miles per hour.
## Fay
[Tropical Storm Fay](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/archive/2020/FAY.shtml?) formed on off the coast of North Carolina on July 9. The storm is about 40 miles east-northeast of Cape Hatteras, North Carolina with winds of 45 miles per hour moving north at 7 miles per hour.
> Tropical Storm Fay ... the earliest 6th Atlantic named storm formation on record. Previous record was Franklin in 2005 on July 22nd.
>
> Source: @[philklotzbach](https://twitter.com/philklotzbach/status/1281326862183079948) on Twitter

## Gonzalo
[Tropical Storm Gonzalo](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/archive/2020/GONZALO.shtml?) formed from Tropical Depression 7 this morning (July 22) about 1250 miles east of the southern [Windward Islands](https://maps.app.goo.gl/rBApgyAM4eY2nxtZ7) with winds of 45 miles per hour moving west-northwest at 12 miles per hour.
> Gonzalo is the [earliest seventh named tropical storm on record](https://twitter.com/philklotzbach/status/1285678664399704064) to form in the Atlantic basin, according to Dr. Phil Klotzbach, a tropical scientist at Colorado State University. The previous record was held by Tropical Storm Gert, which developed on July 24, 2005.
>
> Source: [Weather.com](https://weather.com/storms/hurricane/news/2020-07-22-tropical-storm-gonzalo-forecast-atlantic)
Tropical Storm Gonzalo is expected to become a hurricane by Thursday as it moves toward the Caribbean Sea.
\*\*UPDATE 7/23/2020 2100 UTC\*\*: Tropical Storm Gonzalo is located about 810 miles east of the Southern Windward Islands with winds of 60 miles moving west at 13 miles per hour.
\*\*UPDATE 7/24/2020 2100 UTC\*\*: Tropical Storm Gonzalo is located about 390 miles east of the Southern Windward Islands with winds of 40 miles per hour moving west at 18 miles per hour.
\*\*UPDATE 7/25/2020 2100 UTC\*\*: Remnants of Gonzalo are located about 125 miles west-northwest of Trinidad with winds of 35 miles per hour.
## Hanna

[![](https://res.cloudinary.com/teamsiems/images/w_150,h_150,c_fill,g_auto/v1603993185/TD8-20200722/TD8-20200722.jpeg?_i=AA){: .img-fluid }](https://res.cloudinary.com/teamsiems/images/v1603993185/TD8-20200722/TD8-20200722.jpeg?_i=AA)

[Tropical Depression 8](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/archive/2020/EIGHT.shtml?) formed Wednesday (July 22) about 530 miles east-southeast of Port O'Connor, Texas with winds of 30 miles per hour moving west-northwest at 6 miles per hour. The depression is expected to produce 2 to 4 inches of rain with isolated maximum totals of 6 inches along the Gulf coast from Louisiana to the Lower Texas coast, and inland through south-central Texas and the Rio Grande Valley.

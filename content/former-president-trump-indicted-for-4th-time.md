<!-- wp:paragraph -->

Former President Donald Trump and 18 others were indicted on August 14 on charges related to 2020 election subversion in the state of Georgia.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:more -->  
<!--more-->  
<!-- /wp:more -->

<!-- wp:quote -->

> <!-- wp:paragraph -->
>
> These state charges were brought by Fulton County District Attorney Fani Willis, an elected Democrat who has been investigating Trump’s interference in the election since early 2021.
>
> <!-- /wp:paragraph --><cite>Source: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/interactive/2023/08/georgia-indictment-defendants-list-dg/)</cite>

<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

Two questions keep coming up: 1) could he serve as president if convicted, and 2) could he pardon himself?

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->

> <!-- wp:paragraph -->
>
> The Constitution has very few requirements to serve as President....It does not bar anyone indicted, or convicted, or even serving jail time, from running as president and winning the presidency.
>
> <!-- /wp:paragraph --><cite>Source: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2023/08/15/politics/could-trump-serve-pardon-president-convicted/index.html)</cite>

<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

Most legal experts point to the 14th Amendment as a way to block Trump from serving: if he's convicted of insurrection.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

As for self-pardoning, well it's never been tried before.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->

> <!-- wp:paragraph -->
>
> Self-pardoning remains an open question because no president has tried it. The Constitution, though, doesn’t expressly forbid it in writing.
>
> <!-- /wp:paragraph --><cite>Source: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2023/08/15/politics/could-trump-serve-pardon-president-convicted/index.html)</cite>

<!-- /wp:quote -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

If you're keeping score, that's 91 charges across four indictments.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

**Update 8/25/2023**

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

Trump and other co-defendants were arraigned yesterday at the Fulton County jail in Atlanta. Trump's inmate number is P01135809.

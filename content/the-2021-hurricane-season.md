This year [CSU again predicted](https://teamsiems.com/2021/04/prediction-for-2021-hurricane-season/) an above-average season with 17 named storms, 8 hurricanes, and 4 major hurricanes. If [last year](https://teamsiems.com/2020/05/the-2020-hurricane-season/) was any indication we should expect a wild season that starts early and ends late.
From the [National Hurricane Center](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/), the 2021 Atlantic [tropical cyclone names](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/aboutnames.shtml) are: [Ana](#Ana), [Bill](#Bill), [Claudette](#Claudette), [Danny](#Danny), [Elsa](#Elsa), [Fred](#Fred), [Grace](#Grace), [Henri](#Henri), [Ida](#Ida), [Julian](#Julian), [Kate](#Kate), [Larry](#Larry), [Mindy](#Mindy), [Nicholas](#Nicholas), [Odette](#Odette), [Peter](#Peter), [Rose](#Rose), [Sam](#Sam), [Teresa](#Teresa), [Victor](#Victor), and [Wanda](#Wanda).
This year, instead of the Greek alphabet, the World Meteorological Organization (WMO) published [a list](https://public.wmo.int/en/media/news/supplemental-list-of-tropical-cyclone-names-raiv) of normal names to use as the supplemental list of tropical cyclone names: Adria, Braylen, Caridad, Deshawn, Emery, Foster, Gemma, Heath, Isla, Jacobus, Kenzie, Lucio, Makayla, Nolan, Orlanda, Pax, Ronin, Sophie, Tayshaun, Viviana, and Will.
## Ana
Well the season started early and with a whimper: [subtropical storm Ana](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/archive/2021/ANA.shtml?) formed in the Atlantic today (May 22) about 200 miles northeast of [Bermuda](https://goo.gl/maps/CHGXG7UdzZJPaWPP9). This marks the 7th year in a row a named storm arrived before June 1.

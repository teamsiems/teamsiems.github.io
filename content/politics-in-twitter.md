I was checking the followers list on twitter and look who's following me now, [@GovPerry2010](http://twitter.com/GovPerry2010). It seems Governor Rick Perry - or his campaign manager - started a new twitter account for the 2010 elections. I started following [@GovernorPerry](http://twitter.com/GovernorPerry) and [@TeamKay](http://twitter.com/TeamKay) a few weeks ago just to keep tabs on Rick and Kay. So, apparently, twitter is the new battle ground for politics. Can they get votes from twitterers? Will we ever use [twitter polls](http://www.google.com/search?q=twitter+polls&sourceid=navclient-ff&ie=UTF-8&rlz=1B2GGGL_enUS202US204) to cast our votes? Probably not - not after the dangling chad incident and alleged Texas voting machine tampering\*.

*\* Texas used Austin-based vendor Hart InterCivic model [eSlate](http://www.verifiedvotingfoundation.org/article.php?id=6638). [A report commissioned by the State of Ohio](http://www.usenix.org/event/evt08/tech/full_papers/butler/butler_html/) in 2007 stated that Hart machines were vulnerable to attack (tampering).*

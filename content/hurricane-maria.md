In the past 24 hours, Maria became a hurricane, and strengthened to Category 4. (See [Saffir-Simpson scale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson\_scale).) It's currently next to [Dominica](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Dominica/@15.2987216,-61.17116,7z/data=!4m13!1m7!3m6!1s0x0:0x0!2zMTXCsDA2JzAwLjAiTiA2MMKwNDInMDAuMCJX!3b1!8m2!3d15.1!4d-60.7!3m4!1s0x8c14d2faf2155a15:0x49b39109053afd3c!8m2!3d15.4364722!4d-61.3552094), and tracking north west. Maria is predicted to follow a similar path as [Hurricane Irma](http://teamsiems.com/2017/09/hurricanes-irma-jose-katia/) - passing over [Puerto Rico](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Puerto+Rico/@18.2693582,-68.6483475,7.5z/data=!4m13!1m7!3m6!1s0x8c0296261b92a7f9:0xf336ec2818049b1a!2sPuerto+Rico!3b1!8m2!3d18.220833!4d-66.590149!3m4!1s0x8c0296261b92a7f9:0xf336ec2818049b1a!8m2!3d18.220833!4d-66.590149) - and turning north, staying in the Atlantic.
Also in the Atlantic, Hurricane Jose continues to weaken as it tracks north; still affecting tides along the east coast of the U.S. and Bermuda.

This year <a href="https://twitter.com/philklotzbach/status/1512066915841282048" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">CSU again predicted</a> an above-average season with 19 named storms, 9 hurricanes, 4 major hurricanes.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

Not to go too far off on a tangent, but in 2020 CSU predicted 16 named storms, 8 hurricanes, 4 major hurricanes. Four months later they revised these numbers to be 24 named storms, 12 hurricanes, 5 major hurricanes! We ended 2020 with 30 named storms, 14 hurricanes, 6 major hurricanes!

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

In 2021 they predicted 17 named storms, 8 hurricanes, 4 major hurricanes. We ended 2021 with 21 named storms, 7 hurricanes, 4 major hurricanes.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

From the <a href="https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">National Hurricane Center</a>, the 2022 Atlantic <a href="https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/aboutnames.shtml" rel="noopener" target="_blank">tropical cyclone names</a> are: [Alex](#Alex), [Bonnie](#Bonnie), [Colin](#Colin), [Danielle](#Danielle), [Earl](#Earl), [Fiona](#Fiona), [Gaston](#Gaston), [Hermine](#Hermine), [Ian](#Ian), [Julia](#Julia), [Karl](#Karl), [Lisa](#Lisa), [Martin](#Martin), [Nicole](#Nicole), Owen, Paula, Richard, Shary, Tobias, Virginie, Walter.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:more -->  
<!--more-->  
<!-- /wp:more -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

The Atlantic hurricane season runs from June 1 to November 30. This year we came close to having Hurricane Agatha slide across Mexico from the Pacific and become a tropical system in the Atlantic basin before June 1, but alas it did not happened as of this posting.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

**Update 8/4/2022**

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

NOAA has updated their predictions for this hurricane season.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->

> <!-- wp:paragraph -->
>
> NOAA’s update to the 2022 outlook — which covers the entire six-month hurricane season that ends on Nov. 30 — calls for 14-20 named storms (winds of 39 mph or greater), of which 6-10 could become hurricanes (winds of 74 mph or greater). Of those, 3-5 could become major hurricanes (winds of 111 mph or greater). NOAA provides these ranges with a 70% confidence.
>
> <!-- /wp:paragraph --><cite>Source: <a href="https://www.noaa.gov/news-release/noaa-still-expects-above-normal-atlantic-hurricane-season" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">NOAA.gov</a> taken August 4, 2022</cite>


<!-- /wp:preformatted -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

Julia strengthens to a Hurricane about 10 miles south of San Andress Island, Columbia with winds 75 miles per hour moving west at 17 miles per hour.

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

**Update 10/9/2022 at 0715 UTC**

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:preformatted -->

``` wp-block-preformatted
...JULIA MAKES LANDFALL IN NICARAGUA NEAR LAGUNA DE PERLAS...
```

<!-- /wp:preformatted -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

Julia makes landfall about 30 miles north-northeast of Bluefields, Nicaragua with winds of 85 miles per hour moving west at 16 miles per hour.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

**Update 10/9/2022 at 1800 UTC**

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:preformatted -->

``` wp-block-preformatted
...JULIA MOVING QUICKLY ACROSS NICARAGUA, APPROACHING THE PACIFIC 
COAST...
...RISK OF LIFE-THREATENING FLASH FLOODS AND MUDSLIDES ACROSS
CENTRAL AMERICA AND SOUTHERN MEXICO CONTINUES THROUGH TUESDAY...
```

<!-- /wp:preformatted -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

Julia weakened to Tropical Storm is located about 20 miles north-northeast of Managua, Nicaragua with winds of 60 miles per hour moving west at 16 miles per hour.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

Julia made it across Nicaragua to the Pacific Ocean where it turned north and traveled along the coast (El Salvador) to Guatemala. There it weakened below a depression about 10/10/2022 at 1500 UTC.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

Julia was the third storm to cross Central America and to reform in another ocean. Hurricane Agatha traveled across southern Mexico, dissolved and reformed in the Gulf of Mexico - eventually strengthening to Tropical Storm Alex north of the Bahamas. Tropical Storm Bonnie (2nd Atlantic storm) traveled west across Nicaragua and reformed in the Pacific Ocean.

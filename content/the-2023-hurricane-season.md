The European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts (ECMWF) \[via Colorado Statue University (CSU) meterologist Philip Klotzbach\] predicted that <a href="https://twitter.com/philklotzbach/status/1643605029419380743" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">El Nino will peak</a> to the middle of summer/hurricane season. Apparently this will negatively impact this year's hurricane season. In their <a href="https://twitter.com/philklotzbach/status/1646513575454998535" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">annual tweet</a> CSU predicted a slightly below-normal hurricane season with 13 named storms, 6 hurricanes and 2 major hurricanes.

From the <a href="https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">National Hurricane Center</a>, the 2023 Atlantic <a href="https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/aboutnames.shtml" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">tropical cyclone names</a> are: [Arlene](#Arlene), [Bret](#Bret), [Cindy](#Cindy), [Don](#Don), [Emily](#Emily), [Franklin](#Franklin), [Gert](#Gert), [Harold](#Harold), [Idalia](#Idalia), [Jose](#Jose), [Katia](#Katia), [Lee](#Lee), [Margot](#Margot), [Nigel](#Nigel), [Ophelia](#Ophelia), [Philippe](#Philippe), [Rina](#Rina), [Sean](#Sean), [Tammy](#Tammy), Vince, Whitney.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

The Atlantic hurricane season runs from June 1 to November 30, and as of June 1, there has been very little activity in the Gulf or the Atlantic.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- /wp:heading -->

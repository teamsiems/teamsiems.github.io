A century seems like a long time - it is - but did you ever notice the speed with which technology grows in 100 years; it grows at an increasing rate. The U.S. [Public Broadcast Service](http://www.pbs.org/) and The [History Channel](http://www.historychannel.com/), among others, give us a portal to the past - using the latest technology - to view what was high-tech in the first decade of the 20th century.
[![](http://teamsiems.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/kittyhawk19031-150x150.jpg "kittyhawk1903")](http://teamsiems.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/kittyhawk19031.jpg)In 1903 "powered flight" was the buzzword. The [Wright brothers](http://www.pbs.org/wgbh/nova/wright/) made history with the first, [documented](http://www.wrightexperience.com/edu/1903a/creation/ED.htm) powered flight. Their glider with a motor weighed just a couple hundred pounds and finally flew 59 seconds by the end of their trials; a successful flight by Wilbur's standards.
Wright Brother's historic flight courtesy of Library of Congress. [![Posted by Hello](http://photos1.blogger.com/pbh.gif)](http://www.hello.com/)
In 2005 a consortium of European companies named [Airbus](http://www.airbus.com/) showed the public a monster of a craft, the [Airbus A380](http://www.airbus.com/dynamic/media/press\_releases.asp#1659).
> The 555-seat A380 passenger aircraft has a range of up to 8,000nm/15,000km, allowing it to fly non-stop between Europe and Asia. \[It's\] powered by Trent 900 engines from Rolls-Royce or GP7200 engines from The Engine Alliance. - Airbus website 2005.

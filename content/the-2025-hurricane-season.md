We’re starting this post early this year with the [annual tweet](https://x.com/philklotzbach/status/1907795220374188228) from Philip that calls for a above-normal year with 17 named storms, 9 hurricanes, and 4 major hurricanes. He adds that a “relatively warm Atlantic and likely absence of [#ElNino](https://x.com/hashtag/ElNino?src=hashtag_click) are the primary reasons.”

[NHC](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/)

## References

- From the [National Hurricane Center](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/), the 2025 Atlantic [tropical cyclone names](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/aboutnames.shtml) are: [Andrea](#Andrea), [Barry](#Barry), [Chantal](#Chantal), [Dexter](#Dexter), [Erin](#Erin), [Fernand](#Fernand), [Gabrielle](#Gabrielle), [Humberto](#Humberto), [Imelda](#Imelda), [Jerry](#Jerry), [Karen](#Karen), [Lorenzo](#Lorenzo), [Melissa](#Melissa), Nestor, Olga, Pablo, Rebekah, Sebastien, Tanya, Van, Wendy.
- The Atlantic hurricane season runs from June 1 to November 30.
- The [Saffir-Simpson scale](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/aboutsshws.php) runs from 75 miles per hour to 200 miles per hour.

## Andrea
\*\*6/24/2025 at 1500 UTC\*\*
[Tropical Storm Andrea](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/archive/2025/ANDREA.shtml?) formed about 1205 miles west of the Azores with winds of 40 mph and moving east-northeast at 17 mph.
\*\*Update 6/24/2025 at 2100 UTC\*\*
Andrea is about 1110 miles west of the Azores with winds of 40 mph moving northeast at 20 mph.
\*\*Update 6/25/2025 at 0300 UTC - LAST\*\*
Andrea becomes a remnant low and is about 985 miles west of the Azores with winds of 35 mph moving east-northeast at 20 mph.
## Barry
\*\*6/29/2025 at 1500 UTC\*\*
[Tropical Storm Barry](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/archive/2025/BARRY.shtml?) formed about 90 miles east-southeast of Tuxpan, Mexico, with winds of 40 mph moving northwest at 6 mph.
\*\*Update 6/30/2025 at 0300 UTC\*\*

\*\*Update 6/30/2025 at 0900 UTC - LAST\*\*

On Sunday, July 21, 2024, President <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Biden" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Joe Biden</a>, aged 81, announced he will no longer seek reelection to a second term. Instead he will endorse his Vice President, Kamala Harris. She is the first female vice president and the highest-ranking female official in U.S. history, as well as the first African-American and first Asian-American vice president.

There are 28 days to the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Democratic_National_Convention" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Democratic National Convention</a> and 106 days to the election. Some have suggested the the DNC will be open, while Harris has vowed to "earn and win" this nomination.

<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->

It's been 56 years since a president droped out of their reelection race. <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson#Presidency_(1963%E2%80%931969)" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Lyndon B. Johnson</a> dropped out of his reelection race after low numbers in the first primary in New Hampshire, on March 12, 1968; Joe Biden was 25 years old and would graduate Syracuse University Law school that year.

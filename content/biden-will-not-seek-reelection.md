On Sunday, July 21, 2024, President [Joe Biden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Biden), aged 81, announced he will no longer seek reelection to a second term. Instead he will endorse his Vice President, Kamala Harris. She is the first female vice president and the highest-ranking female official in U.S. history, as well as the first African-American and first Asian-American vice president.

There are 28 days to the [Democratic National Convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Democratic_National_Convention) and 106 days to the election. Some have suggested the the DNC will be open, while Harris has vowed to "earn and win" this nomination.
It's been 56 years since a president droped out of their reelection race. [Lyndon B. Johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson#Presidency_(1963%E2%80%931969)) dropped out of his reelection race after low numbers in the first primary in New Hampshire, on March 12, 1968; Joe Biden was 25 years old and would graduate Syracuse University Law school that year.
\*\*UPDATE 8/6/2024\*\*
Kamala Harris has chosen [Tim Walz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tim_Walz), Governor of Minnesota, as her running mate.

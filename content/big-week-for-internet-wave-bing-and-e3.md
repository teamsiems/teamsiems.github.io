It has been a big week for Internet and gaming technology.

- I follow [Ryan Carson](http://twitter.com/ryancarson) on Twitter and he wrote an article for [thinkvitamin.com](http://thinkvitamin.com/), <a href="http://thinkvitamin.com/dev/six-ways-that-google-wave-is-going-to-change-your-business-career-and-life/" style="font-style: italic;">Six Ways that Google Wave is Going to Change Your Business, Career and Life</a>. It's a good summary of [Google Wave](http://wave.google.com/) which Google presented at [Google I/O](http://code.google.com/events/io/) earlier this week.
- Simultaneous, Microsoft launched [Bing](http://www.bing.com/) - their revitalized search engine (sans Yahoo buyout) - at [All Things Digital](http://d7.allthingsd.com/20090528/d7-interview-steve-ballmer/) (D7).
- Also, [E3](http://e3insider.com/) is <span style="font-style: italic;">happening </span>this week, and [Project Natal](http://www.xbox.com/en-US/live/projectnatal/) (Xbox) literally blew away the competition (Wii/PS3) by eliminating the controller.
- In New York, [Internet Week](http://www.internetweekny.com/), was showcasing several Internet technologies.

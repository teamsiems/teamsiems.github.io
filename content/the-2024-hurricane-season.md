We're starting this post early this year with the [annual tweet](https://twitter.com/philklotzbach/status/1775886040642818296) from Philip that calls for a very active year with 23 named storms, 11 hurricanes, and 5 major hurricanes. He adds that an "extremely warm tropical Atlantic and likely [#LaNina](https://twitter.com/hashtag/LaNina?src=hashtag_click) are the primary reasons."

[NHC](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/)

From the [National Hurricane Center](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/), the 2024 Atlantic [tropical cyclone names](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/aboutnames.shtml) are: [Alberto](#Alberto), [Beryl](#Beryl), [Chris](#Chris), [Debby](#Debby), [Ernesto](#Ernesto), [Francine](#Francine), [Gordon](#Gordon), [Helene](#Helene), [Isaac](#Isaac), [Joyce](#Joyce), [Kirk](#Kirk), [Leslie](#Leslie), [Milton](#Milton), [Nadine](#Nadine), [Oscar](#Oscar), [Patty](#Patty), [Rafael](#Rafael), [Sara](#Sara), Tony, Valerie, William. The Atlantic hurricane season runs from June 1 to November 30. The [Saffir-Simpson scale](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/aboutsshws.php) runs from 75 miles per hour to 200 miles per hour.
## Alberto
\*\*6/19/2024 at 1500 UTC\*\*
[Tropical Storm Alberto](https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/archive/2024/ALBERTO.shtml?) formed about 185 miles east of Tampico, Mexico, with winds of 40 miles per hour moving west at 9 miles per hour.
\*\*Update 6/20/2024 at 1200 UTC\*\*
Alberto made \*\*landfall\*\* near Tampico, Mexico, this morning and continues westward inland. Alberto is located about 25 miles west of Tampico, Mexico, with winds of 45 miles per hour moving west at 13 miles per hour.
\*\*Update 6/20/2024 at 2100 UTC - Last\*\*
Alberto dissapated and is about 260 miles west of Tampico, Mexico, with winds of 30 miles per hour moving west at 24 miles per hour.

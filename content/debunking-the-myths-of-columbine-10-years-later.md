> "Journalist and author Dave Cullen was one of the first to take on what he calls the myths of Columbine. He kept at it for a decade, challenging what the media and law enforcement officials reported." Source CNN.com \| April 20, 2009

> Cullen's book,"[Columbine](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0446546933/ref=cm_sw_r_de_dp)," was released this month -- just in time for today's 10th anniversary of the shooting at the Colorado high school. While tackling popular misconceptions, Cullen also gives a riveting account of what happened that day and how the survivors view the event that marked their lives forever.

Ten years later and we have social networks that [spread news faster](http://teamsiems.com/2009/04/ashton-outmaneuvers-cnn-to-1-million-on-twitter/) than CNN can get to the spot. Imagine - god forbid - if "Columbine" happened today. Networks like [twitter](http://twitter.com/) would spread the word before the incident was even over; remember [Plane Crash in Hudson](http://teamsiems.com/2009/01/plane-crash-hudson-river-flight-1549/).

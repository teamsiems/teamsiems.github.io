Texans Will Soon Carry Guns During Disasters

By chris on 2019-05-27

Earlier this month, the Texas House passed H.B. 1177, a bill that would allow people who are evacuating after a disaster declaration to carry a gun *without a license* up to 7 days. On Friday, May 24, the Senate passed their version which shortens the time period to 48 hours, but allows the governor to extend that time if needed. The new law has to pass the House once more and go to the governor for signature. If signed, it would take effect September 1, 2019. The 86th Legislature runs from Jan. 8 to May 27 (today). As a licensed-to-carry gun owner, I don't know how I feel about letting unlicensed people walking around with guns during a stressful time. (Guns would be allowed in shelters too - if the shelter owner allows it.) I guess the legislature is trying to extend the castle doctrine during a disaster to stop looting and allow people to travel with protection (?).

The Day Computers Stopped

By chris on 2024-07-20

Over night on July 18-19, CrowdStrike quietly pushed out an update to Microsoft computers and servers. By 4 AM CDT, Help Desk Central at Texas A&M University was on alert of a problem campus wide. It seemed computers were stuck in a Blue Screen (of Death) restart loop, and Windows and Azure (cloud) servers were shutting down. Since nearly every Windows computer on campus - including classroom computers - were affected, the University's President cancelled classes, and the IT professionals got to work identifying and fixing every computer they could find with the problem.

The CrowdStrike IT outage forced Texas A&M University to cancel classes Friday as IT professionals manually fixed thousands of impacted devices.

Source: KBTX.com

CrowdStrike is a global corporation, and their security software is in millions of computers world wide.

The global computer outage affecting airports, banks and other businesses on Friday appears to stem at least partly from a software update issued by major US cybersecurity firm CrowdStrike, experts told CNN.

Source: CNN.com

How could such a small update do so much damage? That is the billion dollar question!

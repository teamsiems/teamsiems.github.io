Project Anchor

By chris on 2026-03-18

Eschatology: a branch of theology concerned with the final events in the history of the world or of humankind.

Source: merriam-webster.com

**Project Anchor** is described as a NASA initiative focused on controlling gravitational waves. According to the story, two black holes collided billions of years ago, sending gravitational waves toward Earth. As these waves traveled, they supposedly passed through dark matter, which “lensed” or amplified them. Scientists are said to predict that the waves will reach Earth on **August 12, 2026, at 14:33 UTC (9:33 AM CDT)**, triggering a “gravitational discontinuity event” during which Earth would temporarily **lose gravity for seven seconds**. This raises the question: **Is Project Anchor just another end‑of‑the‑world prediction like the Mayan calendar scare in 2012 or the Y2K panic in 2000, or is there any truth behind it?**

Busted

It's a viral TikTok hoax. On February 12 (6 months before the "event"), I asked Bing Chat (AI) to summarize an the news about Project Anchor. This is what it returned:

Summary of the *topic* (based on established facts)

**1. “NASA Project Anchor” is not real**

There is **no NASA program** called *Project Anchor*. The name appears only in **viral TikTok videos and social‑media posts**, not in any NASA documentation, budget filings, or scientific publications.

**2. The “gravity blackout” claim is a hoax**

The viral claim suggests:

  • Earth will “lose gravity” for a short period
  • NASA warned people to prepare
  • It will cause people to float or collapse

None of this is scientifically possible. Gravity is caused by Earth’s mass. For Earth to “lose gravity,” the planet would need to lose its mass — which cannot happen suddenly or temporarily.

**3. NASA has publicly debunked similar hoaxes before**

NASA routinely clarifies that:

  • Gravity cannot shut off
  • No NASA alert has ever predicted such an event
  • Viral “science” videos often misuse NASA’s name

**4. What *would* happen if gravity disappeared?**

Purely hypothetically:

  • Everything not anchored would fly off the planet
  • The atmosphere would escape into space
  • Oceans would disperse
  • Earth’s orbit and rotation would destabilize

But again — **this cannot happen in reality**.

**5. Why these hoaxes spread**

The article you referenced likely discusses:

  • TikTok videos claiming NASA issued warnings
  • Misinterpretations of NASA’s budget or research
  • Public reactions and jokes
  • Clarifications from scientists debunking the claim
